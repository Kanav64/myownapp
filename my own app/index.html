Different Parks to visit in the United Kingdom

Please visit all these parks and experience the fun in them!

1.CannonHill Park, Mosely

CannonHill park

CannonHill park has a lovely view with plents of activities to do including minigolf, tennis and playing in the themepark. There is also plenty of space to play football, cricket, badminaton and plenty of other sports. The MAC(Midlands Art Center)has amazing food and plenty of shows to watch.

2.Hyde park

Hyde Park

Hyde Park is a Grade I-listed major park in Central London. It is the largest of four Royal Parks that form a chain from the entrance to Kensington Palace through Kensington Gardens and Hyde Park, via Hyde Park Corner and Green Park past the main entrance to Buckingham Palace. The park is divided by the Serpentine and the Long Water lakes.

3.Breacon Beacons National Park

BB National Park

The Brecon Beacons National Park (Welsh: Parc Cenedlaethol Bannau Brycheiniog) is one of three national parks in Wales, and is centred on the Brecon Beacons range of hills in southern Wales. It includes the Black Mountain (Welsh: Y Mynydd Du) in the west, Fforest Fawr (translates as 'great forest') and the Brecon Beacons in the centre and the Black Mountains (Welsh: Y Mynydd Du or Mynyddoedd Duon) in the east.

4.The Cairngorms

Cairngorms Park

he Cairngorms (Scottish Gaelic: Am Monadh Ruadh) are a mountain range in the eastern Highlands of Scotland closely associated with the mountain Cairn Gorm. The Cairngorms became part of Scotland's second national park (the Cairngorms National Park) on 1 September 2003. Although the Cairngorms give their name to, and are at the heart of, the Cairngorms National Park, they only form one part of the national park, alongside other hill ranges such as the Angus Glens and the Monadhliath, and lower areas like Strathspey.
